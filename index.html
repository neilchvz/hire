Origin story

How I got here

My tech journey started with fixing copiers. Seriously. I was a copier technician and solutions specialist for an MPS company and somewhere between tearing apart machines and talking to clients about their workflows, something clicked. I liked solving problems. I liked making things work better.

I originally wanted to play saxophone, become an animator working on hit Japanese animes, and make things with my hands. Turns out I still do, just with infrastructure instead of ink.

I moved to New York deliberately, chose it over San Francisco because of the timezone and because my twin brother beat me there by a few months. I wanted to be somewhere that would push me and NYC delivered.

From there I went the MSP route on purpose. No degree, so I needed somewhere that would give me a chance and throw me in the deep end. MSPs do both. I started at the helpdesk and worked my way up to Director of Projects and Implementations, not by going to school, but by breaking things and fixing them until I understood them.

One thing worth clarifying about that director title, my title changed but the hands-on work never did. I still design and build every day. The Director hat just means I also own the outcomes.

Direction

What I actually want to do

Here's the thing about MSPs, they're a great place to learn everything and a hard place to grow forever. I hit a ceiling. Not a technical one, a structural one. The only path forward was management and honestly? I don't want to manage people. I want to master the craft.

That's when I found the IC path. Senior > Staff > Principal > Distinguished. You can go deep without going into people management.

It also led me to IaC. Before that I knew automation as Rewst, ImmyBot, MDM workflows, reactive, step by step, command driven. Then I found Terraform and the declarative model and it just made sense. Instead of telling infrastructure what to do, you tell it what it needs to be. It figures out the rest. Less human error, less wasted time on the mundane, more energy on the things that actually matter.

Working with me

What I'm like

Ambitious and stubborn, but the good kind of stubborn. The kind that won't drop a problem until it's solved properly. Laid back enough that I won't make a crisis out of everything.

I learn by doing. Always have. Give me something real to work on and I'll figure it out. Give me a classroom and I'll fall asleep.

I believe humans make errors and systems shouldn't depend on humans not making them. I believe automation isn't about replacing effort, it's about redirecting it toward things worth the effort.
